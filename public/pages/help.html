How To Use BirdFeathers

BirdFeathers helps you estimate feed requirements, production cost, expected revenue, and projected profit before you commit money to a flock.

How To Use The Calculator

Step 1

Select the poultry type you want to plan for: broilers, layers, or both.

Step 2

Enter the flock size for the birds you expect to raise.

Step 3

Choose the broiler duration if your estimate includes broilers.

Step 4

Enter chick prices and current feed market prices.

Step 5

Review feed requirements before entering mortality and selling assumptions.

Step 6

Check the calculator and results screens for cost, revenue, and profit.

What To Prepare Before Starting

Flock Size

Your expected number of broilers and/or layers.

Chick Price

Price per day-old chick from your supplier or hatchery.

Feed Prices

Current feed bag prices for starter, finisher, grower, or mash.

Mortality

Expected mortality rate for the flock.

Selling Price

Expected selling price per bird, egg, or crate.

How To Read The Result

Feed Screen

The feed screen shows estimated kilograms and bag quantities needed for the flock.

Calculator Screen

The calculator screen combines chick cost, feed cost, and projected income.

Results Screen

The results screen summarizes the flock outcome and estimated financial position.

Important Note

The profit figure is a planning estimate, not a guarantee of actual farm performance.

Beginner Farmer Resources

Day-Old Chicks

Source chicks from reputable hatcheries with clear vaccination and health records.

Feed

Buy feed from trusted mills, agro-input stores, or suppliers known for consistent quality.

Poultry Inputs

Get drinkers, feeders, vitamins, and medication from established agro-vet stores.

Supplier Checks

Compare suppliers by reliability, bird health, feed quality, and support, not only by price.

Local Guidance

Ask experienced farmers, extension officers, or poultry groups which suppliers they trust in your area.

Important Reminder

This calculator focuses on feed, chick cost, and revenue planning. You should also budget for vaccines, labour, housing, transport, heating, lighting, drinkers, feeders, and other operating costs.
